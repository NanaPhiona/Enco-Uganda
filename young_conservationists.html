Environmental Education and Training Of Young Conservationists In Schools

ENCO's environmental education programs in schools aim to instill a deep undersatnding of and respect for nature among young people. The training emphasizes practical conservation skills, such as how to establish and manage tree nurseries, proper tree planting techniques, and long-term care and maintenance of trees.

Through creation of environmental clubs, students are encouraged to take a leadership role in their schools and communities, organizing activities like clean-up campaigns, tree-planting drives, and nature walks. These clubs serve as a platform for peer education, where students share knowledge about biodiversity, sustainable practices, and the importance of conserving ecosystems.

Additionally, the organisation offers hands-on experiences such as visits to conservation sites, interactions with environmental experts, and workshops on recycling, waste management, and sustainable agriculture. The goal is to empower young conservationists to be lifelong advocates for environmental stewardship and to inspire a new generation of environmentally conscious leaders.
