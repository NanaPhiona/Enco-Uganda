Restoration Of Degarded Areas

ENCO Uganda works on restoring degraded wetlands and forests, aiming to bring these ecosystems back to life and ensure they continue to serve their vital functions for rain formation, biodiversity support, and climate regulation.

To restore degraded areas, ENCO uses the following approaches to achieve that:

  1. Assessment Of Degradation:
    The process begins by evaluating the extent of degradation in an area, including the loss of vegetation, soil fertility, and biodiversity. This helps in planning the most effective restoration strategies
  2. Selection of Indigenous Tree Species
    Choosing the right species is crucial. Indigenous trees are preferred because they are well adapted to the local environment, require less maintenance, and support native biodiversity
  3. Tree Nursery Establishment
    Tree nurseries are set up to propagate indigenous species. This ensures a consistent supply of seedlings for planting.
  4. Community Engagement
    Local communities are involved in the restoration process, from sensitization to active participation in tree planting and maintenance. This ensures that they take ownership of the project and help protect the restored areas.
  5. Planting Techniques
    Trees are planted in a way that maximizes their survival rate. This often includes spacing the tres correctly, watering them reguraly, and using techniques like mulching to retain soil moisture
  6. Continuous Monitoring and Maintenance
    After planting, the restored area is monitored to ensure that the trees are rowing properly. Maintenance activities include watering, removing invasive species, and protecting the young trees from pests and human activity.
  7. Enrichment Planting
    In areas where biodiversity is low, additional tree species may be introduced to enrich the ecosystem and ensure long-term ecological balance.
