Tree Planting And Restoration

One of ENCO Uganda's core activities is Tree Planting and Reforestation, which focuses on increasing tree cover to combat deforestation and environmental degradation. The organisation works with the community members to plant trees in degraded areas and distributes affordable seedlings, ensuring that everyone can participate in restoring the environment.

By planting trees, the organisation not only helps restore degraded forest areas but also contributes to improving the microclimate. This is particularly important for a region heavily dependent on agriculture, as a healthy tree cover can improve soil fertility, stabilize rainfall patterns, and reduce the effects of climate change.

Moreover, reforestation efforts support biodiversity, protect water sources, and provide alternative sources of income through agroforestry. As more trees are planted, both the environment and the local agricultural sector benefit, aligning with ENCO Uganda's mission of promoting conservation and sustainable livelihoods.

While doing tree planting, ENCO Uganda offers advisory and contract tree planting services to the willing clients which makes the organisation sustainable to the general public.
